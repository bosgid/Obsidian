Doctrine of Creation

God created the entire universe out of nothing; it was originally very good; and he created it to glorify himself.

God created the entire universe (i.e. ‚Äúthe heavens and the earth‚Äù, Gen 1:1) out of nothing. Sometimes the Latin phrase ex nihilo, ‚Äúout of nothing,‚Äù is used; it is then said that the Bible teaches creation ex nihilo. This means that before God began to create the universe, nothing else existed except God himself.

Throughout the Bible it is clear that all things were created by God out of nothing (Ps 33:6, 9; John 1:3; Acts 17:24; Heb 11:3; Col 1:16). This is totally different to man, which requires existing, visible things or materials to create.

The positive side of the fact that God created the universe out of nothing is that it has meaning and a purpose. God, in his wisdom, created it for something. We should try to understand that purpose and use creation in ways that fit that purpose, namely, to bring glory to God himself. Moreover, whenever the creation brings us joy (cf. 1 Tim 6:17), we should give thanks to the God who made it all.

How does the Bible‚Äôs description of creation give special significance to the creation of man? ¬ß

Genesis 2 describes the creation of man, and it is clear from this chapter that Adam and Eve were created differently from the rest of creation. They were created in a more special and personal way.

The special creation of Adam and Eve shows that, though we may be like animals in many respects in our physical bodies, nonetheless we are very different from animals. We are created in God‚Äôs image, the pinnacle of God‚Äôs creation, more like God than any other creature, appointed to rule over the rest of creation. Even the brevity of the Genesis account of creation (compared with the story of human beings in the rest of the Bible) places a wonderful emphasis on the importance of man in distinction from the rest of the universe. It thus resists modern tendencies to see man as meaningless against the immensity of the universe.

Distinguish between the Bible‚Äôs teaching about God‚Äôs relationship to creation and each of the following philosophies: ¬ß

The teaching of Scripture about the relationship between God and creation is unique among the religions of the world. The Bible teaches that God is distinct from his creation. He is not part of it, for he has made it and rules over it. The term often used to say that God is much greater than creation is the word transcendent. Very simply, this means that God is far ‚Äúabove‚Äù the creation in the sense that he is greater than the creation and he is independent of it.

God is also very much involved in creation, for it is continually dependent on him for its existence and its functioning. The technical term used to speak of God‚Äôs involvement in creation is the word immanent, meaning ‚Äúremaining in‚Äù creation. The God of the Bible is no abstract deity removed from and uninterested in his creation. The Bible is the story of God‚Äôs involvement with his creation and particularly with the people in it.

‚Ä¢ Materialism

Materialism is the view that the material universe is all there is and denies the existence of God altogether. This is the most common philosophy of unbelievers today. Christians can fall into believing this philosophy when they spend their lives focused on gaining more possessions and material wealth and living as if there is no God or eternity.

‚Ä¢ Pantheism

The Greek word pan means ‚Äúall‚Äù or ‚Äúevery,‚Äù and pantheism is the idea that everything, the whole universe, is God, or is part of God. But if the whole universe is God, then God has no distinct personality. God is no longer unchanging, because as the universe changes, God also changes. Moreover, God is no longer holy, because the evil in the universe is also part of God.

The importance of human responsibility is also denied. Since everything is God, the goal of an individual should be to blend in with the universe and become more and more united with it, thus losing his or her individual distinctiveness. If God himself (or itself) has no distinct personal identity separate from the universe, then we should certainly not strive to have one either. Thus, pantheism destroys not only the personal identity of God, but also, ultimately, of human beings.

‚Ä¢ Dualism

Dualism is the idea that both God and the material universe have eternally existed side by side. Thus, there are two ultimate forces in the universe, God and matter.

But this would mean there is an eternal conflict between God and the evil aspects of the material universe. If both God and evil have apparently always existed side by side, how can we be sure God will ultimately triumph over evil in the universe? This philosophy would deny both God‚Äôs ultimate lordship creation, and also that creation came about because of God‚Äôs will, that it is to be used solely for his purposes, and that it is to glorify him. This viewpoint would also deny that all of the universe was created inherently good (Gen 1:31) and would encourage people to view material reality as somewhat evil in itself, in contrast with a genuine biblical account of a creation that God made to be good and that he rules over for his purposes.

‚Ä¢ Deism

Deism is the view that God is not now directly involved in the creation. Deism generally holds that God created the universe and is far greater than the universe (God is ‚Äútranscendent‚Äù). Some deists also agree that God has moral standards and will ultimately hold people accountable on a day of judgment. But they deny God‚Äôs present involvement in the world, thus leaving no place for his immanence in the created order. Rather, God is viewed as a divine clock maker who wound up the ‚Äúclock‚Äù of creation at the beginning but then left it to run on its own.

While deism does affirm God‚Äôs transcendence in some ways, it denies almost the entire history of the Bible, which is the history of God‚Äôs active involvement in the world. Many ‚Äúlukewarm‚Äù or nominal Christians today are, in effect, practical deists, since they live lives almost totally devoid of genuine prayer, worship, fear of God, or moment-by-moment trust in God to care for needs that arise.

Why did God create the universe? Was it necessary that he do so? ¬ß

God created us, and the entire universe, for his glory (Isa 43:7). Creation shows his great power and wisdom, far above anything that could be imagined by any creature. One glance at the sun or a little intricate leaf is enough to convince us of the infinite power and wisdom required for creation (Jer 10:12). We also see Scripture pointing towards creation and connecting it with God‚Äôs glory:

Psalm 19:1-2

  1. The heavens declare the glory of God, and the sky above proclaims his handiwork.

  2. Day to day pours out speech, and night to night reveals knowledge.

Revelation 4:11

  1. ‚ÄúWorthy are you, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power, for you created all things, and by your will they existed and were created.‚Äù

God did not need to create the universe - there is nothing that he needs. To deny this would be to deny God‚Äôs independence and imply that God needed the universe in order to be fully God. Rather, we must affirm that the creation of the universe was a totally free act of God. It was not a necessary act but something that God chose to do (Rev 4:11). God desired to create the universe to demonstrate his excellence. The creation shows his great wisdom and power, and ultimately it shows all of his other attributes as well. It seems that God created the universe, then, to take delight in his creation, for as creation shows forth various aspects of God‚Äôs character, to that extent he does take delight in it.

Theistic Evolution (Expanded Definition)

God created matter [with regular properties governed by ‚Äúnatural law‚Äù] and after that [God continued to sustain matter and preserve its natural properties, but he] did not guide or intervene or act directly to cause any empirically detectable change in the natural behaviour of matter until all living things had evolved by purely natural processes [which God actively sustained but did not change].

The biggest issue with Theistic Evolution is that it intentionally denies several events depicted in the Bible written in Genesis. To affirm Theistic Evolution as a truth, one would need to affirm that most of the events in Genesis are written as a metaphor and did not really occur. If this truth is held, then it has serious implications on the rest of the Bible and the message of the Gospel due to how foundational these evens are to our faith and the message we preach. Denying the creation accounts in Genesis as facts will cause the whole narrative of the Bible to unravel.

Denying the historical trustworthiness of the creation events also has serious implications on the trustworthiness of a lot of New Testament Scripture (Matt 19:4-5; Luke 3:38; Acts 17:24, 26; Rom 5:12-19[[Rom 5#17|]];1 Cor 11:8, 15:21-22, 45, 47; 2 Cor 11:3; Col 1:16; 1 Tim 2:13-14; Heb 4:4, 10; Rev 4:11; 10:6).

The main events that are denied are:

  • Theistic evolution claims that Adam and Eve were not the first human beings (and perhaps Adam and Eve never existed).

But Genesis 1, which records the initial creation of different parts of the universe, records the creation of the first human beings (Gen 1:27). Denying the existence of Adam also renders some New Testament Scripture obsolete. The description of the beautiful gift of salvation given through the acts of one man (Jesus) undoing the acts of another man (Adam) that Paul writes about in Romans 5 is nullified if Adam never existed. We also lose hope in our future resurrection that is essential to our faith as described by Paul in 1 Corinthians 15 if Adam never existed and the fall never happened.

This point alone is enough for me to deny Theistic Evolution, but for the sake of completeness, I will provide the other Bible events that Theistic Evolution denies.

  • Theistic evolution claims that God did not act directly or specially to create Adam out of dust from the ground.

But the account in Genesis 2 claims that God made the first man from the dust (Gen 2:7).

  • Theistic evolution claims that God did not directly create Eve from a rib taken from Adam‚Äôs side.

Theistic evolution requires that ‚ÄúEve‚Äù (if there was an Eve) had human parents, but the narrative in Genesis 2 gives a different explanation of how God created Eve (Gen 2:21-23).

  • Theistic evolution claims that Adam and Eve did not commit the first human sins, for human beings were doing morally evil things long before Adam and Eve.

According to theistic evolution, human beings have always committed morally evil deeds, and therefore human beings were sinning for thousands of years before Adam and Eve. But Genesis shows that Adam and Eve committed the first human sins in a world that was perfect and free from human sin (Gen. 3:6). Denying this truth will again nullify a lot of the great work done by Christ‚Äôs redemptive work on the cross, as described by Paul throughout the New Testament.

  • Theistic evolution claims that human death did not begin as a result of Adam‚Äôs sin, for, in that view, human beings existed long before Adam and Eve, and those pre-Adamic humans were always subject to death.

All living things known to evolutionary science eventually die, including human beings, and therefore theistic evolution requires that human beings have always been subject to death, and that human beings were dying long before Adam and Eve existed. But according to the biblical narrative, when God first created Adam and Eve, they were not subject to death. The absence of death when Adam and Eve were created is implied by the summary statement at the end of the sixth day (Gen 1:31). In light of later biblical teachings that death is the ‚Äùlast enemy to be destroyed‚Äù (1 Cor 15:26), as well as the prediction that in the age to come, ‚Äùdeath shall be no more‚Äù (Rev 21:4), the initial ‚Äúvery good‚Äù creation should be understood to imply that Adam and Eve were not subject to death when they were first created.

How can someone hope in an eternity promised by God‚Äôs Word to have no death and at the same time believe that God‚Äôs Word saying there was no death before the fall was just a metaphor and not actually fact? How does that person know if the promise of eternity not having death is not also just a metaphor?

  • Theistic evolution claims that God did not ‚Äúrest‚Äù from his work of creation or stop any special creative activity after plants, animals, and human beings appeared on the earth.

Theistic evolution holds that, after the initial creation of matter, God did not act to change the behavior of matter in the universe to create any living things. This implies that God was not ‚Äúbusy with creation‚Äù through the seven phases of creation. He created once, and the seven phases happened naturally with no intervention. This is what is affirmed by those who proposed model of divinely guided evolution. Does this mean God created matter then rested during the development of the world through the seven phases of creation? Or does this mean that God didn‚Äôt rest at all since there was no particular creative work from which He could rest since creation is all due to God‚Äôs providential work?

Denying God‚Äôs rest after creation also has serious implications on New Testament Scriptures, such as Hebrews 4:4-10[[Heb 4#10|]] which promises us rest from our religious works based on the rest God took at the end of creation. If God did not rest then does the comparison not fall flat and cause us to doubt the promised rest we have been given?

  • Theistic evolution undermines several other important Christian doctrines.

The goodness of God: According to theistic evolution, the earth and its present state is the best kind of earth God could create. By contrast, on a traditional view of Genesis 1-3, the blame for natural evil in the world belongs to Adam and Eve, not God.

The moral justice of God: According to theistic evolution, the earliest human beings that were somehow ‚Äúcreated‚Äù by God‚Äôs use of evolution were sinful human beings, committing morally evil deeds from their earliest existence on earth. But if that is the case, it is hard to escape the conclusion that God himself is responsible for human sin, for he never created sinless human beings who were able to obey him and not to sin.

The atonement: If we deny that sin came into the world through Adam, then the parallel with the unity of the redeemed who are represented by Christ does not work.

The resurrection: If we deny that death came into the world through Adam, then once again the parallel between Adam and Christ in 1 Corinthians 15:21-22 does not work. In this way, theistic evolution undermines the effectiveness of the resurrection to give new life to all who are saved by Christ.

Summary of modern Darwinian evolutionary theory

In modern Darwinian evolutionary theory, the history of the development of life began when a mix of chemicals present on the earth spontaneously produced a very simple, probably one-celled life form. This living cell reproduced itself, and eventually there were some mutations or differences in the new cells produced.

These mutations led to the development of more complex life forms. A hostile environment meant that many of them would perish, but those that were better suited to their environment would survive and multiply. Thus nature exercised a process of ‚Äúnatural selection‚Äù in which the differing organisms most fitted to the environment survived.

More and more mutations eventually developed into more and more varieties of living things, so that from the very simplest organism, all the complex life forms on earth eventually developed through this process of random mutation and natural selection.

  • Evolution has no power to create new genetic information.

Random mutation and natural selection (the two processes essential to Darwinian evolution) do not contain the creative power to generate the new genetic information that is necessary for the creation of new proteins and new forms of life. Stephen Meyer, director of the Discovery Institute‚Äôs Center for Science and Culture in Seattle, points to the work of molecular biologist Douglas Axe, who worked as a researcher at Cambridge University from 1990-2003. Axe concluded that:

‚ÄùFor every DNA sequence that generates a short functional protein fold of just 150 amino acids in length, there are 1077 nonfunctional combinations‚Äî1077 amino acid arrangements‚Äîthat will not fold into a stable three-dimensional protein structure capable of performing a biological function.‚Äù

Meyer puts the number 1077 in perspective by pointing out that there are only 1065 atoms in our entire Milky Way galaxy. Meyer goes on to explain:

‚ÄúEvery time an organism reproduces and generates a new organism, an opportunity occurs to generate and pass on a new gene sequence as well. But during the entire 3.5 billion year history of life on earth, only 1040 individual organisms have ever lived‚Äîmeaning that at most only 1040 such opportunities have occurred. Yet 1040 represents only a small fraction of ten to the 1077‚Äîonly one ten trillion, trillion, trillionth, or 1/1037 to be exact.

And 1 in 1037 is the chance of random mutation producing only one fairly simple new protein structure. (The average protein is much more complex, about 300 amino acids in length, which means that the actual chance of producing a new functional protein is far less than these numbers indicate.) And, of course, the building of new animals would require the creation of many new proteins, not just one. So for all living things to have descended from one primitive, simple life form, it would require that random mutation would produce not one but many thousands of new proteins that would form the building blocks of all the different life forms on the earth. If the chance of producing just one new functional protein is one in ten with thirty-seven zeros after it, then the chance of this happening successfully thousands of times is, for all practical purposes, zero. It is impossible.

  • Nonliving matter does not contain and cannot produce the information necessary for life.

James M. Tour, a synthetic organic chemist, is professor of chemistry, professor of computer science, and professor of materials science and nanoengineering at Rice University. He was named among ‚ÄúThe 50 Most Influential Scientists in the World Today‚Äù by TheBestSchools.org and was named ‚ÄúScientist of the Year‚Äù by ReD magazine in 2013. Based on his experience and knowledge, he has written about how impossible it is for scientists to create a living organism for nonliving chemical matter (as proposed by the theory of evolution). He believes that even if the top minds of the world came together with unlimited money and resources, they would not be able to successfully create a living system with nonliving chemical matter. The possibility is reduced even more for chemistry working on its own with no human intervention. He even goes on to write and say:

‚ÄúThose who think scientists understand how prebiotic chemical mechanisms produced the first life are wholly misinformed. Nobody understands how this happened. Maybe one day we will. But that day is far from today.‚Äù

Stephen Meyer goes on to explain that nonliving matter does not contain the information necessary to produce life. He says,

‚ÄúBuilding a living cell in the first place requires assembly instructions stored in DNA or some equivalent molecule.‚Äù

But where did this information come from? Remember, we are not talking about trivial amounts of information. The sum total of DNA in ourselves‚Äîour genome‚Äîcan be thought of as a book with 3 billion base pairs of DNA. That means that the probability of DNA information occurring by chance is the inverse of vast, or essentially zero. Our conclusion must be that it is impossible for the nonliving raw materials found in nature to have created the information necessary for any living organism. The information is so complex and so vast that it must have come from elsewhere, from an intelligent cause of the information.

  • Creating new life forms would require more changes than just mutations in DNA.

Popular explanations of evolutionary theory can make it seem as though a few random mutations in an organism‚Äôs DNA can explain the creation of new life forms. However, molecular biologist Jonathan Wells explains that additional information outside of DNA called ‚Äúepigenetic information‚Äù is necessary for the creation of a new life form. This information directs much embryo development that determines the placement of organs within the body and the overall body form of a living organism. Epigenetic information is apparently contained in cell membranes and in the final folded shape of proteins, but the exact mechanism for this is not yet known. The fact that such epigenetic information is necessary means that any attempts by supporters of evolution to explain the creation of life from nonliving matter or the mutation from one life form to another must explain even more than the supposed) occurrences of beneficial mutations in DNA.
